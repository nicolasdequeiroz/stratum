How we engage as a partner

The operating relationship

Account management and advisory are part of the engagement — not a separate add-on.

Stratum is built to be a long-term technology partner. That means someone is paying attention to your environment between tickets — looking ahead at lifecycle, risk, budget, and roadmap — and bringing it back to leadership in plain language.

Roadmap reviews

Scheduled conversations about what is coming up — replacements, upgrades, risk items — so nothing surprises the budget.

Lifecycle planning

Devices, servers, network gear, and licenses tracked against end-of-life and replacement windows.

Risk & gap visibility

Security, compliance, backup, and access gaps surfaced before they become incidents.

Leadership briefings

Plain-language summaries leadership can act on — without translating tickets and tooling.

How engagements are sized

Sizing principle

We size around the environment — not the user count.

Pricing by seats is convenient for vendors and inaccurate for clients. Two businesses with the same headcount can have very different operating realities. Stratum sizes around what actually drives the work:

Support demand

How often the team actually needs help, and what kinds of help.

Devices & endpoints

Workstations, laptops, mobile, printers, peripherals — managed across their lifecycle.

Servers & infrastructure

What runs the business — on-prem, cloud, or both — and how much oversight it needs.

Backups & recovery

Scope of protection and recovery expectations relative to operational risk.

Network & site complexity

Single office, multiple sites, field crews, guest networks, OT segments.

Security & risk exposure

Sensitivity of data, customer expectations, insurance and compliance posture.
