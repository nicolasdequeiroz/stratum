# Product Economics and Gross Margin View

## Purpose

This page connects the product line to economics.

The goal is to understand not just what Stratum sells, but what it costs Stratum, what it sells for, and what margin is left.

## Business questions

- What does Stratum sell today?
- What is the hard cost to Stratum?
- What is the current sell price?
- What gross margin is left?
- What work does the product create?
- Should it be kept, bundled, repriced, or retired?

## First-pass model

Start with:

- Product / Service
- Vendor
- Billing Model
- Hard Cost to Stratum
- Current Sell Price
- Gross Margin $
- Gross Margin %
- Notes

## Why this matters

This is required before broader package and pricing decisions can be made.

Without economics, the product line stays conceptual.

With economics, Stratum can decide:

- what belongs in the baseline stack
- what should be sold separately
- what is underpriced
- what creates too much management burden
- what supports the package model best

## Decision outcomes

| Decision | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Keep | Healthy enough to continue as-is |
| Bundle | More valuable inside a package than alone |
| Reprice | Useful, but margin is too thin |
| Retire | Too weak, too noisy, or not aligned |
