# 2. Products and Services We Want to Build Toward

## Structured Managed IT Plans

We need to move away from only thinking in terms of break-fix, hourly support, and small retainers.

The future should be clear managed service plans that customers can understand and that we can deliver consistently.

### Possible future packages

- Essential IT Support
- Managed IT
- Managed IT Plus
- Fully Managed Technology Partner

### What this should solve

- Customers know what they are buying
- We know what we are responsible for
- Support becomes more predictable
- Billing becomes cleaner
- The service team has clearer expectations
- We move from selling hours to selling outcomes

---

## Cybersecurity Packages

Cybersecurity needs to become a defined product line.

This should not just be something we react to when there is a problem. It should be a clear, recurring service that helps customers manage risk, meet insurance requirements, and protect their business.

### Possible future packages

- Cyber Essentials
- Cyber Insurance Readiness
- Managed Security
- Security Maturity Roadmap
- Incident Response Plan

### What this should include

- MFA
- Endpoint protection
- Email security
- Patch standards
- Backup requirements
- Security awareness training
- Incident response planning
- Reporting
- Cyber insurance support

### What this should solve

- Customers understand their risk
- We can explain security in plain language
- We can create recurring revenue
- We can help customers meet insurance and compliance requirements
- Security becomes proactive instead of reactive

---

## Backup, Continuity, and Disaster Recovery Packages

Backups should become their own clear service category.

The customer should not just hear “you have backups.” They should understand whether they are actually recoverable.

### Possible future packages

- Backup Monitoring
- Backup Testing
- Microsoft 365 Backup
- Server Backup and Recovery
- Business Continuity Plan
- Disaster Recovery Readiness Review

### What this should solve

- Customers know what is protected
- Customers know what is not protected
- Recovery expectations are documented
- Restore testing becomes scheduled
- Backup reporting becomes part of the customer relationship
- We reduce false confidence around data protection

---

## Account Management and Client Success

This is a major area we need to build.

The more we communicate with customers in a structured way, the more trust we build and the more work we uncover. This should not be random. It needs a rhythm.

### Possible future services

- Monthly customer status report
- Green/yellow/red technology health summary
- Quarterly business review
- Technology roadmap
- Lifecycle planning
- License review
- Warranty and renewal tracking
- Budget planning
- Client success meetings

### What this should solve

- Customers feel looked after
- We stop waiting for customers to ask
- We identify risk earlier
- We uncover project work earlier
- We increase retention
- We build stronger long-term relationships

---

## Business Systems Implementation

This is where ERP, CRM, AI, automation, and Microsoft 365 improvement can live.

This needs to be framed carefully. We should not just say we “do AI” or “do implementations.” We need to define the business problem we are solving.

### Possible future services

- CRM implementation
- ERP support and implementation
- Microsoft 365 optimization
- SharePoint structure
- Workflow automation
- AI workflow consulting
- Reporting dashboards
- Business process mapping
- System cleanup and permissions review

### What this should solve

- Customers get better use out of the tools they already have
- Customers reduce manual work
- Leadership gets better visibility
- Processes become cleaner
- Systems are implemented with structure instead of chaos

---

## Compliance and Risk Readiness

This should connect closely to cybersecurity, insurance, backups, documentation, and executive reporting.

Many customers will not understand compliance deeply. They will just know that insurance companies, regulators, customers, or auditors are asking harder questions.

### Possible future services

- Cyber insurance readiness review
- Compliance baseline review
- Security policy templates
- Access control review
- Patch compliance reporting
- Backup compliance reporting
- Evidence gathering for insurance or audits
- Risk register or risk summary

### What this should solve

- Customers can answer insurance and compliance questions with confidence
- We help customers prove they are managing risk
- Security becomes tied to business requirements
- We create a stronger advisory relationship

---

## Procurement, Shop, and Device Lifecycle

The shop and purchasing side should be more than a place to buy things.

It should become a controlled way for customers to buy the right technology, standardize their environment, and plan replacements.

### Possible future services

- Standard device catalog
- New employee technology kit
- Hardware lifecycle plan
- License management
- Warranty tracking
- Replacement planning
- Client shop portal
- Approved accessories and bundles

### What this should solve

- Customers stop buying random devices
- Support becomes easier
- Security improves
- Replacement planning becomes predictable
- Purchasing becomes simple and controlled

---

## Vertical-Specific Packages

Eventually, we should package services around the types of businesses we serve best.

Different industries have different pain points. We should not speak to all of them the exact same way.

### Possible verticals

- Manufacturing
- Construction
- Professional services
- Accounting and finance
- Healthcare and clinics
- Distribution and logistics
- Local growing businesses with 10–200 employees

### What this should solve

- Marketing becomes sharper
- Sales conversations become more relevant
- We can speak to industry-specific problems
- Customers feel like we understand their world
