# Executive Advisory Layer

## Purpose

This page explains the advisory layer that sits above core managed services.

Stratum is not only moving toward clearer packages. It is also moving toward stronger stewardship, account ownership, and executive guidance.

## Core idea

The advisory layer helps leadership answer:

- what matters most now
- what should be fixed first
- what can wait
- what the roadmap should be
- what the business should invest in and why

## What this layer includes

- quarterly reviews
- technology roadmap discussions
- budget and lifecycle planning
- security and resilience review
- vendor and platform guidance
- accountability for priorities over time

## How it supports the 5S lens

| Lens | Advisory contribution |
| --- | --- |
| Structure | Turns scattered issues into priorities and plans |
| Security | Helps leadership understand and sequence risk reduction |
| Stability | Prevents surprise failures and unmanaged aging systems |
| Simplicity | Gives leadership a clear view of what matters next |
| Stewardship | Acts in the client’s best interest over time, not just inside tickets |

## Where it lives in the product line

- Lightest form inside Business
- Stronger form inside Secure
- Fully built inside Complete

## Management note

This layer should be treated as a real capability, not just a sales phrase.
