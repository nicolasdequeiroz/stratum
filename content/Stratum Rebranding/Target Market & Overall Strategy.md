# Target Market & Overall Strategy

## Purpose

This page captures the current direction for Stratum's rebrand, website, target markets, and digital go-to-market execution.

## Core strategic direction

Stratum should continue to own the core strategy in house.

That includes:

- positioning
- target market selection
- package structure
- website direction
- sales narrative

This fits the work already completed internally and keeps decision-making aligned with the future direction of the company.

## Brand lens

The brand lens guiding this direction is:

- Structure
- Security
- Stability
- Simplicity
- Stewardship

This is the filter for the website, service model, proposals, sales conversations, and internal decisions.

## Who Stratum is for

Stratum should focus on growing organizations in the structural missing middle: businesses that are too complex for break-fix and too small to justify building deep internal IT.

The fit should not be based on user count first.

It should be based on:

- support usage
- devices
- servers
- backups
- network/site complexity
- security/risk
- account needs

This aligns with the internal package model and with the kind of MSP Stratum is becoming.

## Positioning direction

Stratum should not compete on generic MSP language.

The company should be positioned as a structured, security-aware, dependable technology partner for organizations that need reliable systems, clear accountability, and long-term stewardship.

Working positioning statement:

**Stratum brings structure, security, stability, simplicity, and stewardship to growing organizations that need technology they can trust.**

## Primary website industries

These are the industries Stratum should lead with on the first website build.

### 1. Automotive Dealerships

Why this is primary:

- Stratum already works in this segment, so the positioning is credible now
- strong multi-department operational complexity
- uptime, vendor coordination, support responsiveness, printers, phones, Wi-Fi, and shared systems all matter
- good fit for current and future Stratum

### 2. Medical & Dental Clinics

Why this is primary:

- high dependence on uptime, trust, secure systems, and dependable support
- strong fit for security, backup, and continuity value
- usually limited internal IT depth
- strong alignment with Structure, Security, Stability, and Stewardship

### 3. Law Firms & Legal Services

Why this is primary:

- highly sensitive to downtime, trust, document access, and continuity
- strong need for dependable support and accountability
- good fit for business-facing technology stewardship
- accessible owner / partner-led buying structures

### 4. Construction & AEC Firms

Why this is primary:

- combines office, field, project, mobile, and vendor complexity
- strong fit for site complexity, onboarding/offboarding, and support structure
- aligns well with future project/solution work and account ownership

### 5. Manufacturing & Industrial Operations

Why this is primary:

- high environment complexity across devices, servers, sites, networks, and uptime
- strong fit for structured managed services
- strong alignment with stability, standards, and operational accountability

## Next-wave industries

These are still attractive, but do not need to lead the first website pass.

### Accounting & Bookkeeping Firms

- strong fit for uptime, sensitive data, security, and seasonal continuity pressure
- likely stronger as Stratum develops a more mature Secure / Complete-style offer

### Transportation & Logistics

- good fit for dispatch, warehouse, office, mobile, and multi-role operations
- strong site/device/uptime complexity fit

### Nonprofits & Associations

- good fit where stewardship, simplicity, and dependable support matter
- commercially viable, but not as strong a first-wave market as the core industries

### Real Estate & Property Management

- viable for multi-site, communication-heavy, vendor-heavy operating environments
- better suited as a later-wave industry page

## Separate strategic page

### Private Equity & Portfolio Companies

This should not sit as a normal industry page.

It is better framed as a strategic capability / buyer-type page focused on:

- standardization after acquisition
- technology cleanup and stabilization
- security and risk visibility
- onboarding and transition support
- structured growth support

## Service model alignment

Stratum's package logic should remain aligned to customer usage and environment complexity first, not user count first.

Customer fit should be assessed through:

- support demand
- endpoints
- servers
- backups
- network/site complexity
- licenses
- risk exposure
- account management needs

This supports the internal package structure:

- Stratum Essentials
- Stratum Business
- Stratum Secure
- Stratum Complete

## Future Stratum direction

Future Stratum is moving toward:

- stronger account ownership
- clearer separation between helpdesk and project/solution work
- stronger service management and dispatch structure
- more cybersecurity maturity
- more automation and adoption value in the service story
- less reactive break-fix dependence

That means Stratum should increasingly sell:

- managed operational reliability
- cybersecurity readiness
- recoverability and continuity
- lifecycle planning
- long-term stewardship
- automation and workflow improvement

Stratum should define itself as the structured, security-aware, dependable technology partner for operationally complex growing businesses.

The first website build should lead with:

- Automotive Dealerships
- Medical & Dental Clinics
- Law Firms & Legal Services

- Private Equity & Portfolio Companies
