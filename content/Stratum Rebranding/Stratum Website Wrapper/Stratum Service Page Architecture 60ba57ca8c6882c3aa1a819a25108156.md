# Stratum Service Page Architecture

The goal is simple: these pages should explain what we do in a way that feels clear, credible, and structured. They should not read like generic MSP service pages.

## Core brand lens

- Structure
- Security
- Stability
- Simplicity
- Stewardship

## Overall direction

Stratum should come across as an operational technology partner, not just another IT company.

The pages need to feel:

- calm
- direct
- organized
- business-first
- easy to understand

They should show how Stratum helps a business run with less friction, less risk, and more confidence.

---

## Standard service-page flow

Each main service page could follow the same sequence so the site feels consistent.

1. Hero
2. What this service is
3. Where it applies in the business
4. What Stratum handles
5. How Stratum works
6. What the client gets
7. Proof
8. CTA

## Rules for all service pages

- lead with business meaning, not technical language
- explain the service in plain terms
- keep the sections tight
- avoid repeating the same point three different ways
- connect the service to real operating outcomes
- keep the tone steady and confident

---

# Cross-page notes

## What should stay consistent

- same basic structure across all service pages
- same direct tone
- same business-first explanations
- same focus on outcomes
- same trust block logic
- same CTA philosophy

## What the pages should not do

- overuse jargon
- read like product dumps
- sound like generic MSP copy
- lean on fear-based cybersecurity language
- repeat the same value statement in every section
- talk like a vendor instead of a partner

## Bottom line

These pages should not feel like a list of services.

They should show how Stratum helps a business operate with more structure, less risk, and more confidence.
